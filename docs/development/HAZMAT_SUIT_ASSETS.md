# Hazmat Suit Asset Contract

This document fixes the file names, registry IDs, Blockbench structure, and asset handoff expected by the 3.0.6 Hazmat Suit implementation.

## Registry IDs

Public item:

```text
scp_additions:hazmat_suit
```

Internal compatibility armor pieces:

```text
scp_additions:hazmat_suit_helmet
scp_additions:hazmat_suit_chestplate
scp_additions:hazmat_suit_leggings
scp_additions:hazmat_suit_boots
```

The four armor pieces reuse the removed legacy IDs so old saves can resolve them again. They are implementation details and must not receive public recipes or appear in normal creative tabs.

## Blockbench source

Keep the editable source outside the built resources:

```text
art/model_sources/hazmat_suit.bbmodel
```

Create the project with the GeckoLib Blockbench plugin and set:

```text
File → GeckoLib Model Settings → Model Type: Armor
```

Do not construct a player rig manually. Use the generated armor template and do not rename or reposition its primary armor bones.

## Primary armor bones

Keep the exact primary names generated by the installed GeckoLib Armor template. They are expected to correspond to:

```text
armorHead
armorBody
armorRightArm
armorLeftArm
armorRightLeg
armorLeftLeg
armorRightBoot
armorLeftBoot
```

If the installed plugin generates different primary names, preserve those generated names and report them before export so the renderer can map them explicitly.

## Pivots and parenting

Do not move the primary pivots generated by the armor template. Fit the geometry around the rig instead.

- Head geometry rotates from the neck pivot.
- Arm geometry rotates from the shoulder pivots.
- Leg geometry rotates from the hip pivots.
- Boot geometry remains parented to the corresponding leg/boot bone.
- Backpack, tank, belt, and torso-mounted equipment remain under `armorBody`.
- Mask and visor remain under `armorHead`.

A hose connecting the mask to a torso tank should be split into two overlapping sections: a short head-parented section and a longer body-parented section. Hide their overlap under the collar. A single rigid hose cannot follow both head rotation and torso motion correctly.

## Geometry rules

- Model around the player instead of directly on the skin surface.
- Leave a small clearance to prevent skin clipping and z-fighting.
- Make the hood, sleeves, torso, trousers, gloves, and boots visibly thicker than the player body.
- Use geometry for silhouette-changing parts such as filters, tank, backpack, valves, and hose.
- Use texture for seams, folds, warning labels, scratches, and minor fasteners.
- Do not scale the primary bones to fit the suit; resize and reposition cubes instead.
- One model should support both Steve and Alex arms. The suit sleeves should be wide enough to conceal the narrower Alex arms.

## Texture

The current model may keep its 512×512 working atlas. All additional PBR maps must use the same dimensions as the diffuse texture.

Main armor texture:

```text
src/main/resources/assets/scp_additions/textures/armor/hazmat_suit.png
```

Reserved PBR texture names:

```text
src/main/resources/assets/scp_additions/textures/armor/hazmat_suit_n.png
src/main/resources/assets/scp_additions/textures/armor/hazmat_suit_s.png
```

`hazmat_suit_n.png` is the normal map and `hazmat_suit_s.png` is the specular map. Their presence alone does not activate PBR rendering; renderer/resource metadata support will be connected after the base model works in-game.

Keep asymmetric details on unique UV space. Mirroring is acceptable only for plain fabric regions.

For the first version, make the visor visually reflective but technically opaque. A genuinely transparent visor should later be separated into its own render layer to avoid transparency sorting problems across the entire armor.

## GeckoLib export

Export the model to:

```text

```

A custom animation file is not required for ordinary player movement because the armor renderer follows the humanoid pose. An empty animation resource is already provided at:

```text

```

## First-person overlay

Create a transparent full-screen PNG:

```text
src/main/resources/assets/scp_additions/textures/gui/hazmat_suit_overlay.png
```

Recommended canvas:

```text
1920×1080
```

Keep the center mostly transparent. Place mask frame, visor edges, dirt, scratches, and interior shadows near the border. Do not bake a heavy global tint into the whole image unless the reference specifically requires it.

## Sound assets

The Hazmat Suit uses exactly three OGG files:

```text
src/main/resources/assets/scp_additions/sounds/hazmat_equip.ogg
src/main/resources/assets/scp_additions/sounds/hazmat_remove.ogg
src/main/resources/assets/scp_additions/sounds/hazmat_breathing.ogg
```

Gameplay timing:

- equipping takes 80 ticks, or 4 seconds;
- removing takes 60 ticks, or 3 seconds;
- moving, releasing the required input, or otherwise canceling an action stops its sound immediately;
- a successfully completed action may let a short authored audio tail finish naturally;
- breathing begins after the equip sound has finished and loops while the complete suit remains equipped;
- beginning removal stops breathing immediately; canceling removal restarts it.

`hazmat_breathing.ogg` must have a clean loop point without a click, abrupt volume jump, or long silent tail.

## UI contract

The timed equipment bar is already implemented as a reusable overlay:

- width: 240 px;
- height: 8 px;
- centered;
- yellow gradient;
- no icon;
- positioned 18 px above the current Blink Bar;
- filled from empty to full during the timed action.

The server controls begin, correction, completion, and cancellation through `EquipmentProgressPacket`.
